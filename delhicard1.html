Aiimsdelhi to give cpr training to all its staff to counter heart attack cases

AIIMS has signed an agreement with Indian Resuscitation Council Federation to impart the training. Immediate Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) can double
the chances of survival after cardiac arrest.

AIIMS Delhi has announced plans to train all its staff, including doctors, paramedics, security guards and sanitation workers, with the emergency life-saving procedure called Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) to counter
the rising number of heart attack cases.


“New people who will be joining in future will also be trained and the duration of the course will be four hours for a layperson, 1-day training for paramedics and 2-day training for doctors,” said DrReema Dada, spokesperson
of All India Institute of Medical Sciences Delhi (AIIMS Delhi). .
