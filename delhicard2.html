Permit Lokshahi Marathi channel to begin operations: Delhi High Court to Centre

The petitioners had challenged the Centre’s order passed on January 9 suspending
the operation of the Lokshahi Marathi channel for 30 days.

The Delhi High Court recently directed the Union Government to permit the Lokshahi
Marathi news channel to begin
its operations as the petitioners had taken steps to rectify the defects pointed out by the Centre


A single-judge bench of Justice Subramonium Prasad in its January 25 order noted that the petitioners
— Zora Traders Ltd, Swaraj Marathi Broadcasting LLP, and BahubaliShantilal Shah — are entitled to run the channel. “This Court, while exercising its jurisdiction under Article 226 of the Constitutio
n of India and keeping in view the satisfaction arrived at by the Respondents, is of the opinion that the Petitioners must be permitted to commence the operation of the channel. Resultantly, keeping in view the fact that
15 days have already passed after the Impugned Order dated 09.01.2024 has been passed, this Court is inclined to direct the Union of India to permit the Petitioners to commence the operation of News TV Channel “Lokshahi Marathi” forthwith,” Justice Prasad said.
